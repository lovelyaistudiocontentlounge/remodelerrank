# The Project Content Goldmine Audit

## Turn finished jobs into future calls

Most contractors finish a job, take a few photos, maybe post one before-and-after, and move on.

That project could be doing a lot more.

Your completed work can become:

- A project page on your website
- A Google Business Profile post
- A before-and-after reel
- A neighborhood SEO signal
- A review request
- A sales story for your next estimate
- An FAQ answer for homeowners who are still researching
- A trust-builder for referrals who Google you before calling

This audit will help you look at your last 3 to 5 jobs and find the marketing value you are probably sitting on.

## Project page outline

Use this structure for your website.

### Page title

[Project type] in [city/neighborhood]: Before and After

### Section 1: The problem

Start with why the homeowner called. Keep it simple and specific.

Example:

The homeowner wanted a kitchen that felt more open, had better storage, and finally matched the rest of the home.

### Section 2: The plan

Explain what you recommended and why.

### Section 3: The work

Show the process, timeline, and important decisions.

### Section 4: The result

Show the after photos and explain what changed.

### Section 5: The proof

Add the review, testimonial, or client quote.

### Section 6: The next step

End with a clear CTA.

Example:

Planning a [project type] in [city]? Send us a few details about your project and we’ll help you understand the best next step.

## 10 content prompts from one project

Use these as captions, reels, emails, blog posts, or Google updates.

1. Before I show you the after, here’s what this homeowner was dealing with.
2. This is what changed in this [room/project] and why it matters.
3. The biggest challenge on this project was not what you think.
4. Here’s what affects the cost of a project like this.
5. Here’s how long this project took from start to finish.
6. Three decisions that made this result work.
7. What this homeowner asked before hiring us.
8. Why we chose [material] instead of [alternative].
9. What we would tell anyone planning this same project.
10. The part of this project most people do not see.
