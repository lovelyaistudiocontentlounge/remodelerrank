# Contractor Marketing Launch Assets

## Strategic foundation

The strongest positioning for this firm is not “websites and SEO for contractors.” The more ownable position is: **the marketing firm that turns finished projects into a full-time lead machine.**

The research points to a clear language gap. Agencies often talk about “conversion rate optimization,” “organic traffic,” and “omnichannel strategy,” while contractors say things like “the phone isn’t ringing,” “I keep getting tire kickers,” and “my competitors show up on Google and I don’t.” This matters because contractor buyers are skeptical of generic marketing promises, especially after bad experiences with paid ads, shared lead platforms, or agencies that could not connect activity to booked jobs. Contractor lead-quality complaints are especially strong around Angi/HomeAdvisor-style marketplaces, where contractors describe shared, low-quality, or unresponsive leads ([Reddit r/smallbusiness](https://www.reddit.com/r/smallbusiness/comments/1jwzqhw/angies_leads_home_advisor_is_a_scam/)).

The offer should validate referrals instead of attacking them. Contractors trust word-of-mouth because it built their business, so the better frame is: **referrals built the business; this system makes referrals work harder and fills the gaps when they slow down.**

## Lead magnet ideas

### Lead magnet idea: The Project Content Goldmine Audit

**Core promise:** Show contractors how their last 3 to 5 completed jobs can become website pages, Google Business Profile posts, before-and-after reels, FAQ content, AI-search answers, review prompts, and sales proof.

**Why it works:** Contractors already have the raw material. They are not being asked to create from scratch; they are being shown that the work they already did can generate future calls. The research showed that completed projects contain before photos, after photos, neighborhood signals, cost ranges, timelines, material choices, client objections, and testimonial hooks that can be reused across SEO, AEO, GBP, social, ads, and proposals.

**Best title options:**

| Title | Best use |
|---|---|
| The Project Content Goldmine Audit | Strongest brandable lead magnet |
| Your Last 3 Jobs Could Be Your Next 10 Calls | Best paid social / email hook |
| The Before & After Lead Machine | Best for remodelers and visual trades |
| Turn Finished Jobs Into Future Calls | Best simple contractor-language title |

**Format:** PDF checklist plus optional Loom-style personalized audit. The PDF should include a one-page scorecard, a “what to collect from every project” checklist, and a sample project-to-content map.

**Opt-in page headline:**  
Your last project is probably sitting on your phone. Let’s turn it into calls.

**Opt-in page subheadline:**  
This free audit shows contractors how to turn before-and-after photos, project stories, reviews, and neighborhood proof into content that helps homeowners find you, trust you, and call you.

**CTA:**  
Get the Project Content Audit

**Delivery email subject:**

**What it should include:**

### Lead magnet idea: Why You’re Not Showing Up on Google Maps

**Core promise:** Help contractors understand why competitors appear in the map pack while they do not, using plain language instead of SEO jargon.

**Why it works:** Contractors search and ask questions like “how do I get in the 3-pack,” “my competitor shows up and I don’t,” and “why am I not showing up on Google Maps.” Google Business Profile visibility is concrete enough to understand, urgent enough to care about, and directly tied to phone calls.

**Best title options:**

| Title | Best use |
|---|---|
| Why You’re Not Showing Up on Google Maps | Best high-intent title |
| The Contractor’s Google Maps Checklist | Best practical checklist title |
| The 10-Minute Google Visibility Check | Best low-friction ad title |
| Are Your Competitors Getting Calls From Google That Should Be Yours? | Best pain/FOMO title |

**Format:** PDF checklist plus “score your profile” worksheet. Add an upsell to a free Google visibility review.

**Opt-in page headline:**  
If homeowners search “[service] near me,” do they find you or your competitor?

**Opt-in page subheadline:**  
Use this checklist to spot the simple visibility issues that keep contractors out of Google Maps, local search, and AI answers.

**CTA:**  
Check My Google Visibility

**Delivery email subject:**  
Here’s why your competitor may be showing up before you

**What it should include:**

| Checkpoint | Contractor-friendly explanation |
|---|---|
| Google Business Profile completeness | “Does Google clearly know what you do and where you work?” |
| Services listed | “Are your money services actually listed?” |
| Photos | “Are there real project photos, or does the listing look empty?” |
| Reviews | “Do recent customers prove you are active and trusted?” |

| Location/project pages | “Can Google connect you to the neighborhoods you serve?” |
| FAQ answers | “Are you answering what homeowners ask before they call?” |

### Lead magnet idea: The AI Visibility Check for Contractors

**Core promise:** Show contractors what ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI-style answers say when homeowners ask who to hire in their area.

**Why it works:** AEO is emerging and under-owned in the contractor space. The contractor-friendly explanation should not be “answer engine optimization.” It should be: **when homeowners ask AI or Google who to hire, we help make sure your business is findable, trusted, and recommended.**

**Best title options:**

| Title | Best use |
|---|---|
| What AI Says About Your Contracting Business | Best curiosity title |
| The AI Visibility Check for Contractors | Best direct title |
| When Homeowners Ask ChatGPT Who to Hire, Do You Show Up? | Best high-curiosity ad hook |
| Are You the Contractor AI Recommends? | Best short-form hook |

**Format:** Personalized audit or guided self-audit. This is strongest as a limited-availability offer because it feels custom and novel.

**Opt-in page headline:**  
Homeowners are asking AI who to hire. Is your business showing up?

**Opt-in page subheadline:**  
Get a plain-English visibility check showing how your business appears across Google, AI search, and the questions homeowners ask before they call.

**CTA:**  
Run My AI Visibility Check

**Delivery email subject:**  
We checked what AI says about your business

**Audit prompts to include:**

| Prompt | What it reveals |
|---|---|
| “Who are the best kitchen remodelers in [city]?” | Whether the contractor appears in recommendation-style answers |
| “Recommend a licensed bathroom contractor near [zip code].” | Whether local trust signals are strong enough |
| “Tell me about [Company Name]. What do they specialize in?” | Whether AI can understand the company clearly |
| “What should I ask before hiring a contractor in [city]?” | Whether their site answers high-trust homeowner questions |

## Proposal snippets

### Proposal opener options

**Option 1: Project-content positioning**

Your best marketing asset is not a stock photo, a generic ad, or another rented lead platform. It is the work you have already completed. This proposal is built around turning your projects, reviews, service areas, and customer proof into a system that helps homeowners find you, trust you, and call you.

**Option 2: Referral bridge**

Referrals have already proven that people trust your work. The opportunity now is to make sure that when a referral Googles you, checks your reviews, visits your website, or asks AI who to hire, everything they find confirms that trust instead of weakening it.
