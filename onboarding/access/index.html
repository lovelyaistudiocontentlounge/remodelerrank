Google Business Profile
5 min
1

Go to your Google Business Profile

Open a browser and go to your Google Business Profile dashboard.

business.google.com

Sign in with the Google account you used to create your business listing. If you are not sure which account, search Google for your business name and look for the "Edit Profile" button.

2

Open the menu and go to Business Profile Settings

In the left sidebar, click on the three-dot menu or "Settings." Select "Managers."

Screenshot: Business Profile Settings → Managers menu
3

Add Jennifer as a Manager

Click "Add" or the "+" button. Enter Jennifer's email address and set the role to Manager (not Owner).

hey@remodelerrank.com

Manager access lets Jennifer post updates, respond to reviews, and edit your profile — but cannot delete the listing or remove you as Owner.

4

Confirm the invitation

Click "Invite." Jennifer will receive an email and accept within 24 hours. You will get a notification when access is confirmed.

✓

You stay in control. You are the Owner. Jennifer is a Manager. You can see everything she does and remove access at any time from the same Managers screen.

Confirm when done

Jennifer's email added as Manager (not Owner)
Invitation sent — Jennifer will confirm receipt
Google Ads
5 min
ⓘ

Only complete this section if you are running Google Ads. If you do not have an active Google Ads account, skip to the next section.

1

Sign in to Google Ads

ads.google.com

Use the same Google account connected to your campaigns.

2

Go to Admin, then Access and Security

In the left menu, click the wrench icon (Tools and Settings). Under "Setup," select "Access and Security."

Screenshot: Tools and Settings → Access and Security
3

Invite Jennifer with Standard access

Click the blue "+" button. Enter Jennifer's email and select the role Standard.

hey@remodelerrank.com

Standard access lets Jennifer view performance, edit campaigns, and manage bids — but cannot add or remove other users or change billing.

4

Send the invitation

Click "Send Invitation." Jennifer will accept within 24 hours.

Confirm when done

Jennifer added as Standard user in Google Ads
Invitation sent

Facebook Page
4 min
1

Go to Meta Business Suite

If you manage your Facebook Page through Meta Business Suite, use that. Otherwise, go directly to your Facebook Page.

business.facebook.com
2

Open Page Settings

In Meta Business Suite: click Settings in the left menu, then "People." On a regular Facebook Page: go to your Page, click "Settings," then "Page Roles" or "New Pages Experience."

Screenshot: Meta Business Suite → Settings → People
3

Invite Jennifer as an Editor

Click "Add People" or "Add New Page Role." Enter Jennifer's email and select Editor.

hey@remodelerrank.com

Editor can post, respond to messages, and view insights. Editor cannot delete the Page or add/remove admins.

4

Save and confirm

Click "Invite" or "Save." Jennifer will accept within 24 hours.

Confirm when done

Jennifer added as Editor to your Facebook Page
Invitation sent
Instagram
2 min
ⓘ

Instagram access is managed through Meta Business Suite — the same place as Facebook. If you completed Step 4 above and your Instagram is connected to your Facebook Page, Jennifer may already have access. Confirm below.

1

Confirm your Instagram is a Business or Creator account

Open Instagram, go to Settings, then "Account." Make sure the account type is Business or Creator — not Personal. Personal accounts cannot be managed by agencies.

2

Connect Instagram to your Facebook Page (if not already)

In Meta Business Suite, go to Settings, then "Instagram Accounts." Connect your Instagram account to your Page. Once connected, Jennifer's Editor access from Step 4 covers Instagram as well.

Screenshot: Meta Business Suite → Settings → Instagram Accounts

Confirm when done

Instagram is a Business or Creator account
Instagram connected to Facebook Page in Meta Business Suite
Yelp
3 min
1

Sign in to Yelp for Business

biz.yelp.com

Use the email address you used to claim your Yelp listing. If you have not claimed it yet, let Jennifer know and she can help you claim it first.

2

Go to Account Settings, then Manage Users

In the top-right menu, click your account name, then "Account Settings." Select "Manage Users."

Screenshot: Yelp for Business → Account Settings → Manage Users
3

Add Jennifer as a Manager

Click "Add User." Enter Jennifer's email and select Manager.

hey@remodelerrank.com

If your Yelp listing is not yet claimed, skip this step and mention it on the onboarding call — Jennifer will walk you through claiming it.

ⓘ

Yelp's Manager role lets Jennifer respond to reviews and update your business information. Yelp does not offer an agency-style access system like Google does, so the Manager role is standard.

Confirm when done

Jennifer added as Manager in Yelp for Business (or listing flagged as unclaimed)
